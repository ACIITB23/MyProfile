Projects

Bachelor's project

Project title: A Project Report on Standardization of herbal drugs (Trikatu)

Project supervisor: Mrs. Geeta Rai

Summary: As part of my undergraduate final semester thesis, I undertook a project focused on standardizing an herbal drug known as Trikatu. Specifically, I worked on standardizing and validating the Ayurvedic formulation called "Trikatu Churna." To achieve this objective, we conducted various wet lab experiments to assess the quality and purity of the herbal drug. Additionally, we also investigated the nature of any adulterants present, utilizing parameters such as morphological, microscopic, physical, chemical, and biological analysis. Our study revealed that the herbal drug formulation adhered to the standards outlined in the Ayurvedic Pharmacopoeia of India. As a result, we concluded that these formulations are therapeutically safe and can be effectively utilized in the treatment of various ailments. Overall, our project contributes to the broader effort of enhancing the reliability and efficacy of Ayurvedic medicines through standardized processes and validation procedures.

Master's project

Thesis Title: Antioxidant Therapies to Oxidative stress-related Neurotrophins Involved in Alzheimer’s Disease

Supervisor: Dr. Satyavani Guttula

Summary: My Master's research is focused on the role of antioxidants and their defense mechanisms in oxidative stress implicated in Alzheimer's disease. The study utilized in silico methods to identify the binding affinity of antioxidants against neurotrophins, and ADMET analysis was performed to examine the pharmacokinetic parameters of these antioxidants. The docking results of our study revealed that Theaflavin, Catechin, Luteolin, Kaemferol, Gallic acid, Myricetin, Eriodictyol, Uric acid, Ascorbic and Isorhamnetin acid showed the best binding affinity with three different proteins (1NT3, 1BND, 1B8M). This finding suggests that these compounds could effectively reduce oxidative stress and play an important role in reducing the risk or delaying the clinical onset of progressive dementia.

Ph.D. Project

Title: Studies on genetic factors involved in Alzheimer’s disease using an integrated computational approach

Supervisor: Dr. Ashutosh Mani

Summary: Dementia is increasing rapidly in the world population. Alzheimer's disease is an important factor behind the development of dementia in older people. Due to the unclearly known mechanism of pathophysiology and target identification Alzheimer's disease treatment remains a great challenge for modern drug discovery. Only a few drugs are available for the treatment of the disease. There is an urgent need to explore these targets for designing efficient Alzheimer's drugs with minimum side effects. In this study, RNA Seq analysis was performed to identify the differentially expressed genes, and their significant enriched functions with pathways were identified by using an integrated computational approach. On the basis of the literature review survey, a few upregulated genes were selected for further studies. Template-based modelling was performed to generate the 3D structure of upregulated receptors. Molecular docking and molecular dynamics simulation studies of docked complexes followed by pharmacokinetic parameters analysis of compounds helped in identifying promising lead compounds. Analysis of RNA-Seq data in this study was an important source for providing information regarding the significant biomarkers, diagnostic as well as novel therapeutic targets for AD. We conclude that expression of major neurotransmitters is increased due to inverse agonism, however these neurotransmitters are not available to bind with their natural ligands and are preoccupied with misfolded Aβ granules. This condition further disturbs calcium signaling and promotes NFT formation and finally causes neuronal cell death. These results further provide a basis for evaluating and exploring the functions/pathways of DEGs, biomarker development, and identification of novel drug targets in Alzheimer's disease. In the third objective, inhibitors for upregulated genes' proteins were designed. Natural inhibitors of these receptors are prospective therapeutics for Alzheimer's disease because they will not only compete with Aβ granules for binding with GPCRs but also work as potential breakers of the negative agonism loop. Overall predicted targets and screened compounds could be helpful for the AD drug discovery process and the treatment of AD patients. The current in silico study could be suitable for further designing novel inhibitors against target proteins with the help of in vivo and in vitro analysis.

Database

Glucose Binding Proteins Database (Unpublished)

About

The Glucose Binding Proteins Database is a comprehensive collection of information related to proteins that bind to glucose molecules. It aims to provide researchers and scientists with a valuable resource for studying and understanding the various aspects of glucose binding proteins.

Features

  • Curated data on glucose binding proteins
  • Structural information and binding site details
  • Search and browse capabilities

Accessing the Database

Please note that the Glucose Binding Proteins Database is currently unpublished and not publicly accessible. It is under development and undergoing continuous refinement. Researchers and interested parties may contact the database administrators for more information or potential collaborations.
